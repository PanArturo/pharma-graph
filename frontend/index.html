⚠ Demo Limitations
  • Payment records capped at 500 per category (7 categories × 500 rows). Full dataset for large states may contain 50,000+ records.
  • Physicians from 5 specialties only (NPI Registry has 800+).
  • 38 therapeutic conditions mapped to ICD-10 codes via keyword matching.
  • OpenFDA drug labels unavailable for medical device companies.
Methodology
Data Sources
NPI Registry
Physicians are fetched from the CMS National Provider Identifier registry by state and specialty. Five specialties are queried: Cardiology, Endocrinology, Internal Medicine, Oncology, and Neurology.
CMS Open Payments
Pharma-to-physician payment records from the federal Open Payments database. Seven payment categories are queried (consulting fees, speaker honoraria, travel, education, food, grants, and other compensation), up to 500 records each.
OpenFDA
Drug brand names and indications are fetched from FDA drug label records for each pharma company found in payments. Company names are normalized and searched with multiple fallback variants. Medical device companies return no results and are excluded from drug nodes.
Node Types
Pharma — a company that made at least one payment. Size scales with log(total paid).
Physician — a doctor who received at least one payment. Size scales with √(total received).
Drug — a brand-name drug from FDA labels associated with a pharma company.
Device / Product — a medical product named in a payment record that didn't match a known drug.
Condition — an ICD-10 therapeutic area parsed from FDA drug label text using 38 keyword patterns.
Edge Types
PAID — pharma → physician. Weight = total USD transferred. Multiple payments from the same company to the same doctor are summed. Edge width and particle speed scale with payment amount.
MANUFACTURES — pharma → drug or device. Created when a company has a matching FDA label or named product in a payment record.
INDICATED_FOR — drug → condition. Created when a drug's FDA label text matches a condition keyword.
SPECIALIZES_IN — physician → condition. Created based on specialty-to-condition mapping (e.g. Cardiology → Atrial Fibrillation, Heart Failure).
RECEIVED_FOR — physician → drug or device. Created when a payment record names a specific product.
PEER_OF — physician ↔ physician. Two doctors share the same specialty and both appear in the payment data. Capped at 400 edges.
Payment Calculation
All dollar figures come directly from CMS Open Payments records and represent the reported payment amount in USD. Where multiple payments exist between the same company and physician they are summed into a single PAID edge. The total displayed in the stats bar is the sum of all visible PAID edge weights and represents a sample — the full year dataset may contain significantly more transactions.
Graph Layout
The 3D layout is computed by a force-directed simulation. Nodes repel each other while edges act as springs. Repulsion strength scales with node count so sparse datasets (device-heavy years) spread out as much as drug-heavy ones. Node caps: 200 physicians, 80 drugs, 60 devices. Edge cap: 1,200 total, with structural edges prioritised over PAID, and PEER_OF capped last.
